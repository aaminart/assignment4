Review

The "Titanic" movie, released in 1997 and directed by James Cameron, is a romantic disaster film that revolves around the ill-fated maiden voyage of the RMS Titanic. The story follows the fictional characters Jack Dawson, a penniless artist, and Rose DeWitt Bukater, a young aristocrat, who fall in love aboard the ship.

Plot

The "Titanic" movie tells the story of a young and wealthy woman, Rose, who boards the maiden voyage of the luxurious ship. Feeling suffocated by her aristocratic life, she meets and falls in love with Jack, a free-spirited artist. Their romance blossoms against the backdrop of the opulent ship. However, their love faces obstacles, primarily Rose's engagement to a wealthy man, Cal. As the ship encounters an iceberg and begins to sink, Jack and Rose fight to survive amid the chaos and mounting danger. The movie intricately weaves their love story with the tragic disaster of the Titanic, depicting the heart-wrenching struggle for survival and the sacrifices made for love.
